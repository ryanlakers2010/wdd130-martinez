Information About This Topic

A 50,000-Year Legacy

Humans have been creating music for approximately 40,000 to 50,000 years. Music has become an essential part of human culture through religious hymns, musical plays, songs, and instrumental performances[cite: 2]. Music has also inspired books, dances, and even movies[cite: 2].

The Power of Emotion & Personal Experience

Why has music become such an important part of our lives? One reason is its ability to express personal experiences and emotions[cite: 2]. One example I enjoy is a song by Olivia Rodrigo from her album "GUTS."[cite: 2] In many of her songs, she shares personal experiences and emotions from her life[cite: 2]. This allows listeners to connect with her music and understand the feelings she is expressing[cite: 2]. That is the power that music can have[cite: 2].

The AI Emergence

Recently, AI has become capable of producing music through computer programs[cite: 2]. It analyzes music that has already been created and uses patterns to generate new songs[cite: 2]. Even though AI can create music faster than a human, many people believe it lacks one key ingredient: the passion, emotions, and personal experiences that make music meaningful and special[cite: 2].
